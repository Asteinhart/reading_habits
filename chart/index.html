Book Over the Years

Follow my reading journey from February 2016 to January 2024.

By Austin Steinhart | Spring 2024

As a child I was a voracious reader, if only to rack up Accelerated Reader points (iynyk). However, my reading plummeted in high school. In 10th grade, I watched more movies in class than books I read and in my 12th-grade literature class, I managed to read only two of the required ten-plus books I was assigned.

College brought back my love for reading in due to my two closest friends were English majors and wouldn't shut up about it. Their passion for books must have reinvigorated my love for reading as the first summer after college, i dove back into reading by setting an ambitious goal of reading a book a week over the summer (which i accomplished!). After this impassioned reentry into reading, I have considered myself a generally consistent reader.

I by no means consider myself someone who reads a crazy amount of books but I do consider myself someone who will jump on any chance to collect copious amounts of personal data about myself.

Scroll along below as I explore the reading over the last eight years.

I have tracked the date I finished every book I have read since 2016 on Goodreads (yes I know I should switch to StoryGraph). I did not track the start date but since I rarely read more than one book at a time, I imputed a start date using the completion date of the last book I had read. This methodology appears to exaggerated my average reading rate. For example, it may appear it took me 30 days to read a book but in actuality, it may have taken a week but I did not start the book until 3 weeks after I had finished the most recent book.

This chart shows every book I have read from February 2016 to January 2024. Each blue bar represents one book. The length of the bar signifies how long I spent reading the book and the bars move up vertically to signify the total number of books I have read since February 2016.

We will be zooming into five different time periods to further explore my reading over the years.

In the summer of 2016, I challenged myself to read a book a week in an attempt to revive my reading which was for the most part a successful! Exploring a possible career in law and working in my hometown prompted me to pickup titles related to race and socioeconomic factors. Almost all the books I read were nonfiction, a trend we will see persist.

After this jump-start, I had a hot streak in which I read 1.8 books a month in the first half of college with most of the reading happening during breaks. During the second half, my reading pace declined and I only 0.4 books a month. It must have been the dread of the unknown ahead slowing me down.

As I settled into full time work and the COVID-19 pandemic began, I miraculously had my most consistent period of reading. During this time, I read 3.2 books a month and read 47 books in 2020, the most in year to date.

During this period, 75% of the books I read were nonfiction which matches the overall split between fiction and nonfiction for 2016 to 2024.

In October 2021, I started a new job and soon after moved to New York City in the start of 2022. Through all this transitions, my reading took a bit of hit as I read only 1.2 books a month.

Outside of a small slowdown in the fall of 2022 in which was I applying to graduate school, my reading picked back up in 2022 and 2023 as I read 1.9 books a month.

One notable development in this period is that I got my first ebook in July of 2023. I rode the high horse as a physical-book-type-person but when I found out an ebook fits in my Uniqlo crossbody bag and that, it was hard to argue with the convenience.

As I allude to, in the fall of 2023 I began graduate school and generally was able to keep a decent pace, reading 1.7 books a month. However, I almost certainly will not be able to maintain this pace for much longer.

And that brings us to the end of our journey. Thank you for following along as I explore my reading over the last few years. Feel free to continue to explore the books by howering over the bars.
